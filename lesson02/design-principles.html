Design Principles Showcase

Google homepage

Google is a classic example of Hick's Law. Hick's Law can be applied to optimize the presentation of choices and information to enhance the overall user experience. Google's homepage is a classic example of simplicity and minimalism. The main focus is on the search bar, keeping choices to a minimum. Users can quickly perform a search without distractions.

YouTube

YouTube is a great exemple of Fitt's Law. Fitts's Law is a principle in human-computer interaction and ergonomics that predicts the time required to rapidly move to a target area, like a button or a link, based on the size of the target and the distance to it. Youtube video screen use the Fitts's Law by making the play button for the featured video largeand central. This prime location and ample size make it easy for users to tap and start watching,minimizing the time and effort required to reach the desired action.

Apple

Apple is an exemple of Rule of Thirds. It involves dividing an image or design into a grid of equal parts. The key elements or points of interest are then placed along these lines or at their intersections.Apple's mobile product pages utilize the Rule of Thirds by prominently displaying the hero imagein two-thirds of the screen, off-center to add dynamism. The key features and call to action are then positioned at the intersections of the thirds, drawing the user's eye to the most important information and action point.
